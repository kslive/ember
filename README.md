## What is it

**Ember** is a desktop app for Mac that captures microphone and system audio during a
meeting, turns it into a transcript in real time, and then prepares a structured summary
(TL;DR, decisions, action items).

Everything happens **on-device**: speech recognition runs on a local Whisper model, notes
are generated by a local AI model. Nothing is sent to the cloud.

---

## Features

- **🎙️ Recording & live transcript.** Microphone and system audio are blended by a
  professional mixer (RMS ducking, clipping protection); speech is recognized locally and
  shows up on screen in real time — with a live equalizer and timer.
- **🏠 One-click start.** Record button or the **⌘R** hotkey.
- **📞 Automatic call detection.** Ember watches which apps are using the microphone
  (Zoom, Google Meet, Teams, FaceTime, Telegram, etc.): **a call begins — recording starts
  on its own; the call ends — recording stops automatically** and moves to processing. It
  works in the background even when the Ember window is minimized — no meeting slips by.
- **✦ One-click summary.** A finished meeting is turned into notes by a local AI model:
  TL;DR, key decisions, and action items with owners. Built-in models (Qwen3, on Apple MLX)
  and external providers (Ollama, Claude, OpenAI, OpenRouter) are supported.
- **🔎 Search** across transcripts and summaries of every meeting, with match highlighting.
- **🎨 Themes** — light / dark / auto (follows the system).
- **🧭 Menu-bar icon** — start/stop and quick access, even when the window is hidden.
- **⚡ GPU acceleration** (Metal + CoreML) for fast transcription.
- **⬆️ Automatic updates** — Ember checks for new versions and installs them in one click.
- **💾 Save meeting audio** to MP4 (optional).

## Installation

> Requires **macOS 14+** on **Apple Silicon** (M1/M2/M3…).

1. Download `Ember_1.2.0_aarch64.dmg` from the [**Releases**](../../releases/latest) page.
2. Open the `.dmg` and drag **Ember.app** into your **Applications** folder.
3. The app is ad-hoc signed (not notarized by Apple), so macOS will block it on first
   launch. Remove the quarantine in one of these ways:
   - **Right-click** `Ember.app` → **Open** → **Open** again in the dialog; **or**
   - in Terminal:
     ```bash
     xattr -dr com.apple.quarantine /Applications/Ember.app
     ```
4. On first launch, go through onboarding: grant access to the **Microphone** and **System
   Audio Capture** (via the buttons on the permissions screen) and download the
   speech-recognition model (Whisper) and the AI model for summaries.

## Privacy

Ember is designed to be **privacy-first**:

- Recording, transcription, and summarization run **locally**.
- Audio and text are stored only on your Mac.
- No accounts, no meeting telemetry, no cloud (unless you choose an external AI provider for
  summaries yourself).
